II. The Divine Covenant

Hestia, Goddess of Flame and Hearth

Hestia is a being made entirely of fire. She possesses every flame that has ever existed, including the one she created: the Golden Flame, a fire that dissolves the ego — the constructed self, the sustained false narrative, the lie a person maintains about who they are. In a cosmos where the most dangerous enemy operates through the systematic manipulation of identity, Hestia's creation is the natural predator of the entire Mavarran civilization. The Golden Flame does not search or discriminate. It reaches for what is false. If truth exists underneath, the person survives, healed. If nothing exists underneath — if the subject is hollow or clings to the false self past the point of severance — the Flame continues through the mind and everything it is composed of, reducing the whole to ash. This outcome is called Sarcax.

To imagine Hestia, imagine Sol'Kheret ten times larger and composed of fire itself. She is the goddess of the hearth — the fire at the center of the home, the one the family gathers around — and she is as terrifying as she is warm. The two qualities are not in tension. She is a mother who loves her children completely and will let them fail, because failure is how one learns. She watches her people endure the Trial of Flame and feels pride. She watches them build shelter for strangers and feels pride. She watches them sit in silence for a year absorbing nothing but filtered sunlight and feels pride. Three expressions of her fire. The diversity is the design.

Her blessing to the Pyrtheran people is Solar Synthesis: the metabolic ability to convert stellar radiation into vitality, myst, and emotional resilience. This is the same class of divine gift as Mavarra's Pansilion, Salacia's Krenides, or Edgerinne's blessing of flesh-metal integration. It is the patron's gift to her people, the biological foundation of everything the culture became. Under seven suns, it renders them functionally inexhaustible.

III. The Exvisar

Sol'Kheret — The Sun Blossom

Sol'Kheret is the most beloved Exvisar in Pyrtheran history. Her titles include Warden of the Seven Suns, Torchmother of Becoming, the Ember Queen, and Invictar Solis — the Unconquered of the Sun. She is a Shaman Queen: a sovereign who governs her civilization and a shaman who channels the goddess Hestia through her own body. She sees the kafer — the visible spiritual aura — of those before her, perceives the interior state of hearts and egos, and administers the Golden Flame as a means of mystical healing through spiritual intervention.

She is a former Templar who underwent the Branding of the Golden Flame — the full application, the one that leaves most recipients mindless. It hardly changed her. The Flame went through and found so little falsehood that she emerged almost exactly as she entered. Then Hestia descended and accepted the formalized ritual duel that determines the Exvisarship: fourteen days of the full Sacred Braid — fighting, dancing, lovemaking — with the goddess, while keeping every torch in the court lit. One passage for each of the seven suns. She maintained every torch for the duration. She is Exvisar not because she survived a fight with a goddess, but because she fought a goddess and still tended the fire.

In appearance: dark-skinned, statuesque, hair braided with sunfire and gemstones, seven pupils in her eyes. Her body is the most extensively branded in the realm. In her Court — the roofless obsidian amphitheater called the Court of Becoming — she sits on the floor because the floor is already hers. The drums, the torches, the dueling, the cooking, the branding, the tantra — these are the instruments of her shamanic practice and the texture of daily life and the community's gathering and the spiritual maintenance of her people, all at once, on the same floor, under open sky. When she raises a hand, the drums stop. When the drums stop, the world stops with them.

She has been at war with Tenebralis for the duration of her reign. She is the only member of the Exvisarelim with her sights set on the Realm of Shadows — not as a political adversary but as a spiritual sickness she can see with the clarity the Golden Flame grants her. The limited trade agreements she maintains with Thalassor and Zephyros exist almost entirely to keep one specific outpost supplied. Beyond that, she wants nothing from the other realms and does not consider them allies.

IV. The Land

Seven Suns

Seven stellar bodies occupy staggered orbits above Pyrthera. A minimum of three are visible at any hour. At peak alignment, all seven blaze simultaneously. For any non-Pyrtheran organism, the heat constitutes active lethality: exposed skin burns within minutes, vision blurs, respiration becomes the sensation of inhaling from an open kiln. Survival requires continuous myst-shielding — expensive, draining, imperfect. For Pyrtherans, the same radiation is food. More suns means more energy. At peak alignment, they describe euphoric saturation: every sense sharpened, the boundary between physical pleasure and spiritual clarity dissolved. Sol'Kheret dueled Hestia for fourteen days because her biology permits it.

The ambient soundscape is the interior of a fire. Roaring heat, crackling stone, wind, the buzz of solar-fed insects, distant combustion. Pyrtheran vocal volume is a biological calibration to this environment — their baseline speaking register must carry over a world that crackles at ambient level.

Three principal zones occupy the main continent, connected by the River of Flames — a magma flow that runs from the Ashen Vale through Khet and down into the Freeholds of Rhylos. This is the central artery of Pyrtheran civilization: the lifeblood of the world made literally visible as molten rock.

Khet sits in the middle: open desert under the full unobstructed blaze of all seven suns. Crimson dunes, black glass mesas, oasis-citadels of sandstone and obsidian. Rhylos occupies the lowlands where the River of Flames spreads into the Ignition Rift — canyons split by magma rivers, volcanoes in sustained eruption, settlements on basalt arches spanning lava flows. The Ashen Vale occupies the high ground — dormant volcanic mountains where suspended ash filters the suns into perpetual amber twilight, cities carved into mountain interiors. Other continents and islands exist but are sparsely populated. Three million people on an entire planet means most of the land is wild, sun-scorched, and occupied by the solar-fed megafauna that constitute the planet's apex ecology.

Pyrthera maintains no walls, checkpoints, or immigration infrastructure. The seven suns are the only border it has ever required.

V. The Three Nations

Under the Hestian Faith

The Emberate of Khet
Becoming Through Trial — Fire as Judgment and Memory
Geography

The open desert at the center of the main continent, under the full unobstructed blaze of all seven suns. Crimson dunes, mesas of black glass, canyons where light is inescapable. Oasis-Citadels built around natural wells and myst-springs, their architecture sandstone and obsidian, roofless, every surface angled to catch and hold light. Public spaces are floored in mirror-polished obsidian that reflects the suns upward. The Court of Becoming — Sol'Kheret's roofless amphitheater — is here.

School of Magic

The Path of the Golden Crucible. Solar myst channeled as transformative heat, both physical and metaphysical. The Ignis Judicium — the Golden Flame — is the foundational art: a metaphysical solvent that burns ego, not flesh. Solar Forging imbues objects with solar myst. The Aegis of the Seven Suns projects shields of solidified sunlight. The most common personal combat application is setting one's fists aflame or breathing fire — fire-breathing is a difficult and respected technique requiring sustained lung capacity, and a true fire-breather is considered extraordinary at home and abroad.

Life

Khetians are hunters. The megafauna of the desert are solar-fed reptilian apex predators of enormous scale — creatures other realms would classify as dragons. A Khetian hunt is a coordinated tactical engagement against prey that is as fast, as inexhaustible, and as dangerous as the hunters themselves. Horse-sized reptilian hunting companions are bonded to families across generations — they sleep against the house wall and keep it warm, and children grow up climbing on them. Post-hunt bonfires are the primary mechanism of civic life: communal cooking, dueling, debate, music, courtship, drinking, and wagering, all occurring simultaneously. These gatherings last for days.

Visual Details

Maximum exposed dark skin. Minimal firesilk wraps in golds and reds. Golden brands at highest density. Hunter's belts carrying weapons and crystata orb-bombs. Solar-absorptive bone from great kills worked into tools and weaponry. The loudest and most immediately legible Pyrtheran silhouette. When the broader cosmos uses the word "Pyrtheran," they typically mean Khetian.

Ten people carrying a carcass that blazes with refracted light across the dunes at sunset, drums already building behind them, children running ahead, someone heating the stone slabs. Tonight will be enormous.

The Freeholds of Rhylos
Becoming Through Flow — Fire as Covenant and Ecology
Geography

The lowlands where the River of Flames spreads into the Ignition Rift. Canyons and plateaus split by rivers of magma, volcanoes in sustained gentle eruption. The air shimmers with heat haze and carries the low rumble of the earth. Settlements built on massive basalt arches spanning the lava flows, or carved into cliff faces. The architecture is organic — cities that appear grown from the rock rather than constructed, shaped by the Dance of the Molten Heart. The Obsidian Citadels built for the Glacian refugees are here: cold inside, designed for someone else's comfort.

School of Magic

The Dance of the Molten Heart. Geothermal myst channeled through percussive, grounded movement to influence magma flow, shape stone, and synchronize group action to the planet's geological pulse. Magma-Weaving, Stone-Shaping, and Heart-Drumming are the primary applications. The art is relational — fire is not commanded but partnered with. The magma dancers who built the Glacian citadels practice this school.

Life

Pastoral. Rhylians herd volcanic-adapted megafauna that follow magma flows the way terrestrial animals follow water. The herder reads the flows and guides the herd through safe corridors between active eruptions. This is mutualism, not domestication — if the herder reads the flow wrong, the magma does not wait and the animals do not care. The Dance of the Molten Heart was developed as a survival necessity before it became a theology. Rhylians are also the realm's master architects and stonemasons.

Visual Details

Denser firesilk draping than Khet, in earthtone golds and volcanic reds. Builder's tools alongside weapons. Bodies shaped by stonework. Architecture spanning rivers of glowing magma. The Obsidian Citadels — given freely, cold inside, asked for nothing.

A man shifts his drumming near a woman at the fire. Not for her. Near her. She either moves toward the rhythm or she doesn't. The community sees. Nobody speaks.

The Ashen Vale
Becoming Through Insight — Fire as Perception and Potential
Geography

Dormant volcanic mountains where suspended ash filters the seven suns into perpetual amber twilight. Cities carved into mountain interiors, illuminated by bioluminescent fungi and geothermal vents. The light is softer here — diffused, contemplative, settling on the skin like memory. The River of Flames originates in these highlands before descending through Khet and into the Ignition Rift.

School of Magic

The Ash-Sight, formally called Cinerix. The art of reading the residue of combustion — perceiving futures, pasts, and the truth of souls from the patterns left in sacred ash. Where the Golden Crucible burns away the false and the Molten Heart shapes the new, the Ash-Sight reads what fire has already done. Key applications include Ember-Vision (perceiving the kafer and interior state of another), Cinder-Recall (retrieving the heat of a past moment from its residue), and Ash-Cloak (reducing one's spiritual visibility to near-imperceptibility).

Life

The people of the Ashen Vale do not eat. They have refined Solar Synthesis until conventional nutrition is unnecessary. The ash-filtered light is dimmer, but sufficient for people who have learned to need almost nothing. This is not deprivation — it is sufficiency. Their asceticism represents the most radical theological position within Pyrthera. They supply the realm's most sought-after oracles and strategists and serve as neutral mediators between Khet and Rhylos.

Visual Details

The one Pyrtheran silhouette that conceals: robed, covered, the concealment itself the statement. Pale ashen amber eyes. The fewest brands of the three nations, though those they carry tend to be the oldest and the hardest to read. A stillness that unsettles even other Pyrtherans.

Two people meditating near each other in a bioluminescent cave. A shared silence that becomes specific. Everyone around them knows something has begun.

VI. The People

Standing in the Sun

The Pyrtheran phrase "standing in the sun" describes the practice of being fully present for whatever the moment contains — whether it is destroying you or filling you with joy. The suns do literally rejuvenate them; the phrase is simultaneously physiological instruction and spiritual philosophy. Pain is sacred. Pleasure is sacred. Neither diminishes the other. The person who can hold both at once, without using the good to numb the bad or the bad to dismiss the good, is the person who has Become.

Pyrtherans are loud — biologically calibrated to carry over an environment that sounds like the interior of a fire. They are direct — not from ideological commitment to candor but because lying is physiologically detectable through the kafer and socially futile in a population of three million where reputation is face-to-face. They are deeply spiritual people — shamanic, passionate, indomitable. They worship Hestia and the seven suns with an intensity that can appear at a glance like sun-worship itself, and Sol'Kheret in a room functions as something close to an eighth sun.

They are, demonstrably, the happiest people in the cosmos. Their spiritual practice produces the result it claims to produce and you can see it on them. They are also a people at war, and in a population this small over a thousand years, everyone knows someone who has served at the Hearth of Luxania. The bonfire at home means what it means because they know what the dark looks like. They choose the light not as a response to the dark but because the light was always what they were.

Physical Transformation

Generations of Solar Synthesis have produced a population that is dark-skinned (maximizing solar absorption), statuesque, and shaped by lifetimes of continuous physical engagement. Reflexes and perception are directly fueled by solar intake. Under seven suns, they are inexhaustible — capable of sustained activity for days without rest. The golden brands glow against dark skin under sunlight: a body illuminated by its own history. The kafer — the heat-haze corona of spiritual state — radiates from every Pyrtheran body and is legible to every other Pyrtheran. Eyes burn in the red-gold myst spectrum: precise judging gold in Khet, deep shifting magma-red in Rhylos, pale ashen amber in the Vale.

In a room full of people from other realms, a Pyrtheran is immediately distinct. Warm spectrum in a cool-spectrum room. Branded skin in semi-translucent firesilk. The wrong volume, the wrong amount of visible skin, the wrong palette. They do not adjust, because adjustment would require performance, and performance is dishonesty.

VII. Schools of Magic

The Threefold Fire

Three schools corresponding to the three nations, each representing a distinct claim about what fire is. All draw on the same solar myst. All are legitimate expressions of the Hestian faith. Sol'Kheret is the only living practitioner acknowledged to have mastered all three.

The Path of the Golden Crucible (Khet)

Fire as judgment and memory. The Ignis Judicium — the Golden Flame — is the foundational art. A metaphysical solvent that burns ego and leaves truth. Additional arts include Solar Forging and the Aegis of the Seven Suns. The most common personal combat expressions are flame-wreathed fists and fire-breathing. Fire-breathing requires exceptional lung capacity and sustained control; a true fire-breather is rare and celebrated. This school produces the branding ritual and the Trial of Flame.

The Dance of the Molten Heart (Rhylos)

Fire as creation and circulation. Geothermal myst channeled through percussive, rhythmic movement. Magma-Weaving, Stone-Shaping, Heart-Drumming. The most physically grounded school. Responsible for Pyrthera's architecture, including the Glacian citadels.

The Ash-Sight / Cinerix (Ashen Vale)

Fire as information. Reading the residue of combustion: what the spark reveals and what the ash remembers. Ember-Vision, Cinder-Recall, Ash-Cloak. The most subtle school. Prolonged practice produces a stillness and detachment that other Pyrtherans find eerie.

The Black Flame

Carried by one being: Tein-Enhotep, the Last Androsphinx. The Black Flame is the only fire in existence that truly destroys matter and energy. What it touches is gone — not transformed, not reduced, not burned to ash. Gone. Permanently removed from existence. Tein-Enhotep breathed it five thousand years ago to annihilate the Androsphinx species, which had been the apex predators of Pyrthera, keeping the human population in the position of prey. He has not used it in millennia. He has not met a foe that warranted it.

Antaka

The ability to collapse the distance between a planet and its sun — to force a star out of its orbital position and bring it into contact with a world. Six people hold this power: Sol'Kheret and her five Torchbearers. A seventh — the Archangel Fiacre — was taught the art by Sol'Kheret during a diplomatic visit, in what was framed as a drunken contest of strength but functioned as a declaration of trust. Antaka is not a weapon. It is an extinction event. Its existence constitutes nuclear deterrence at planetary scale.

VIII. Fashion & Material Culture

The Body Receives the Light

Skin is a solar panel. Covering it blocks the fuel source. Everything a Pyrtheran wears exists to magnify light, secure a tool, or display the brands. Firesilk — a glass-fiber weave, semi-translucent and prismatic — is the base textile. Under multiple suns it shifts and refracts along its threads. Dyed in golds, reds, and greens for solar absorption, or left colorless to illuminate the brands beneath. In Pyrthera it is common and practical. In Thalassor it is a luxury import sold as "warmth without weight."

The silhouette is minimal: sashes, wraps, bands. Draped saree-like construction — one shoulder bare, midriff exposed, legs visible in motion. Belts carry tools and weapons. Bangles and armbands of gold-toned metal. Firesilk strands woven into hair alongside rubies, emeralds, and crystata. The body is visible through semi-translucent fabric. No armor under any circumstances — armor blocks sunlight, reducing speed, reflexes, and myst regeneration.

Status reads through brands first, then through quality of metalwork, gems, and firesilk. A merchant-warrior's daughter carries crystata orbs on her belt — her family's trade goods repurposed as incendiary weapons, swaying at her hip. Poverty looks like less ornamentation but not less exposure. Even the poorest Pyrtheran stands in the sun with their brands visible, because brands are free and light is free.

IX. Weapons & the Relationship to Force

The Personal Art

Every Pyrtheran develops a personal martial identity. A shared vocabulary of fundamental techniques exists, but how an individual combines and deploys them under pressure is theirs alone — as personal as their brands, as unique as their history of Becoming. Copying another practitioner's style is a form of spiritual dishonesty. The closest external analogy is mixed martial arts: a shared framework within which every fighter constitutes a unique tactical problem.

Weapons are chosen through preference and trade. Mastery of multiple weapons is standard. The most common magical combat expression is not ranged projection but close-quarters fire: fists set aflame or fire breathed at proximity. A Pyrtheran fight is intimate by nature — the fire comes from the places where contact and breath happen.

External military conflict is rendered largely theoretical by Antaka. Internal disputes are resolved through personal combat between the principals. A ruler who sends a champion rather than fighting personally accepts permanent reputational cost. Duels are the primary civic mechanism for dispute resolution — officiated, spectated, wagered upon, accompanied by musicians and communal cooking. The duel resolves the dispute; the gathering around it feeds the community.

Pyrtheran mercenaries are the most sought-after combatants in the universe. On a battlefield, a Pyrtheran presents as unarmored, sun-fed, dressed for a celebration, and moving at a speed non-solar-adapted opponents cannot track.

X. Relationship to the Other Realms

The Thousand-Year Verdict

When Luxania fell — when Erebus murdered Aether, God of Light, and the Tenebrali occupation began — Pyrthera showed up. Angels descended and informed the Exvisar. Torchbearers carried the torch to Luxenfall and established a sanctuary — a Hearth — at Thavriel, the sky-city of the Astral Navigators, the Luminacy that had originally discovered the connections between the realms. Thavriel sits on a floating island accessible only by the Skysteps. It was chosen for the strategic advantage of the high ground. The symbolism proved incidental.

Thalassor did not come. The Sinclairs controlled the Clairgates — infrastructure built from stolen Luxanian Astral Navigator technology — and did not open them. Ferridane declared it not their problem: the Luxanians should have built swords instead of light bridges. Zephyros offered intelligence and supplies but would not fight. Pyrthera bled alone.

Then the cosmos sat down at the table with the occupiers. Thalassor resumed trade. The Rewritten — Luxenfallen whose minds had been restructured by Tenebrali Oneironauts — began appearing in Thalassori ports and Ferridanian markets as traded goods. Pyrthera judged this. Not with anger. With the clarity of the Golden Flame applied at civilizational scale. The cosmos was carrying a lie it refused to have burned away. To remain at the table was to become complicit, and complicity in falsehood is the one thing Pyrtheran theology cannot accommodate structurally.

The Hearth has been held for a thousand years. Pyrtherans rotate through Thavriel on active deployment, operating in lightless conditions that starve their Solar Synthesis, fighting rewritten Luxenfallen sent at the walls by the Tenebrali occupation — victims turned into weapons and aimed at the one place still trying to protect their people. Sometimes the weapons are children. The stories the veterans bring home are the stories of every soldier who has tried to save a population while fighting through it. In a community of three million over a millennium, everyone knows someone who has been there.

The Hearth is the only place in the cosmos that still calls the realm Luxania. Everyone else uses Luxenfall — the name of the corpse.

Sol'Kheret's trade agreements with other realms exist to keep Thavriel supplied. Beyond that, she wants nothing. The other Exvisars are, in her assessment: a profiteer (Thalassor), a pragmatist (Ferridane), and a witness who will not act (Zephyros). All three are aware of the situation. All three have chosen their version of inaction. All three have opinions about how Sol'Kheret conducts her war.

XI. Leadership & Governance

The Warbrand and the Sun Blossom

Pyrtheran social structure is a meritocracy of proven excellence: what one has endured, mastered, and given. The hierarchy ascends from Ashbearers (laborers, farmers, craftspeople — the productive ground), through the Perpetually Becoming (mystics on continuous pilgrimages of self-refinement), to Stellarchs (judges and flamewardens who officiate duels and administer minor Trials), to the five Torchbearers (each personally selected and branded by Sol'Kheret, each capable of wielding Antaka, each drawn from a different nation to represent the synthesis), to the Exvisar herself.

The Exvisarship is earned through a specific sequence: surviving the full Branding of the Golden Flame, then defeating Hestia in the fourteen-day duel of the Sacred Braid while maintaining the ceremonial torches. This has been the formal mechanism for as long as the title has existed. Before it existed, Pyrthera was governed by sovereigns of different character — including Tein-Enhotep, who ruled Khet five thousand years ago as a conqueror-king called the Warbrand. His kingdom was the foundation of what Khet is today. The culture Sol'Kheret tends — the Sacred Braid, the feasting, the integration of pleasure alongside pain — represents the evolution of a civilization that began under the Warbrand as a war-forged empire freed from predation by the annihilation of the Androsphinx species.

Tein-Enhotep remains alive and present in Sol'Kheret's Court. The Warbrand and the Sun Blossom coexist: the mountain who cleared the world and the shaman who tends the garden that grew in the clearing. She shows him something like deference. He thinks she is rambunctious. He chalks it up to youth.

XIII. Societal Dysfunction

The Structural Vulnerabilities

Pyrthera's civilization operates on trust — the ambient condition of a population small enough that reputation is personal, honest enough that the kafer makes deception visible, and theologically committed to the Golden Flame's capacity to burn away what is false. Vex Mavarra demonstrated during the Mavarran Incursion that this foundation can be compromised: she appeared in the Court of Becoming, mimicked Pyrtheran behavior, spoke through the mouths of Torchbearers, and proved that assets existed inside the most secure space in the realm. The Golden Flame can burn what is present; it cannot inoculate against what arrives tomorrow. Trust in Pyrthera has shifted from a settled condition to one requiring continuous re-verification, and the act of re-verification is itself corrosive to the trust it seeks to restore.

Simultaneously, Pyrthera bears the suspicion of the broader cosmos for the destruction of Glacia. Everyone in the realms knows that six people hold the power of Antaka. Someone called the sun down on Glacia twenty years ago. The Oracles of Zephyros could exonerate Pyrthera and refuse to do so. The actual perpetrator — Raiwata, the Sun Caller, the Apex Archdevil of Hel — remains unnamed by anyone with the authority to be believed. Sol'Kheret is the cosmos's most correct diagnostician and its most convenient suspect, prosecuting a war against Tenebralis while carrying the unresolved accusation of an atrocity committed by the thing she should be hunting.

The Hearth rotation carries its own cost. A millennium of sending people into a lightless warzone and receiving them back carrying memories that the Sacred Braid can hold but not erase produces a population for whom the knowledge of the dark is universal and permanent. Pyrthera is not broken by this. But it is shaped by it in ways that the theology of Becoming — which holds that both pain and pleasure are sacred — can accommodate without fully addressing.

XIV. The Gonlan and the Trialkeeper

Pyrthera is home to two Gonlan — primordial draconic beings, children of Aracice the Star-Eater. Pyralithe, the Gonlan of the Upper Sky, is the transcendent solar flame above. Agnivar, the Gonlan of the Volcanic Heart, is composed of lava bones and dragon glass — a being of the deep earth. Between them they represent Pyrthera's two elemental registers: the fire that falls from above and the fire that rises from below. Both are in mourning at the start of the series — children of a murdered god, feeling the sickness from Hel spread through worlds their father built.

Residing in Pyrthera but predating its civilization by millennia is Tein-Enhotep, the Last Androsphinx — once called the Warbrand, sovereign of Khet five thousand years ago. He annihilated his own species with the Black Flame to free the Pyrtheran people from predation. His brands are not personal spiritual biography but historical record: entire wars and eras inscribed on his body. Branded down his spine, in a script only the gods can read, is the record of the First War — the foundational conflict of the cosmos, dictated to him by Nyx, Aracice, and Hestia. The gods who won that war sealed Nyx — the only being who could translate the record — to prevent her from reading it to her son Alciel, the Dragonson. The truth of the cosmos has been walking in the open on an Androsphinx's spine for five thousand years, untouchable and unreadable, waiting for a translator who is locked away.

Tein-Enhotep is humorless, immovable, and carries honor for the fallen rather than grief. He trained Alciel in the discipline of the martial. He stands beside the Dragonson as godfather, bodyguard, and keeper of a promise made to his oldest friend: Aracice, the Dragon-God, who asked him to look after the boy. Tein-Enhotep said yes because he is the only person alive who knows what Alciel is about to become. The Last Androsphinx mentoring the Last God. Both carrying the weight of being the final member of a category that used to have more names in it.
